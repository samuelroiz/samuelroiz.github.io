How we got started?

Our journey in dog fostering began in 2022 with our first collaboration with Foxy and the Hounds, a rescue organization whose approach to dog rescue deeply resonated with us. While we worked with several groups, Foxy and the Hounds stood out for their commitment to each dog's well-being and successful placement. Fostering has been a rewarding experience, offering love and rehabilitation to dogs in need, preparing them for their forever homes, and bringing immense joy and fulfillment to our lives.

Meet Sam

"My name is Sam, and I am the creator of this website and one of the two primary fosters here. My passion for dogs and other animals inspired me to venture into fostering. The realization of how many animals are in dire need of homes and the opportunity to place them in loving, wonderful environments fueled my commitment to this cause."

Meet Victoria

"My name is Victoria, and alongside being a dedicated foster, I am a proud guardian to four rescue dogs. My journey into fostering was sparked by a deep-seated passion for dogs and other animals. Witnessing the sheer number of animals in need of nurturing homes, I was compelled to take action. This has led me to not only foster but also to give a forever home to four wonderful rescues, each with their own unique story and place in my heart."

Our rescued dogs

This year, our delightful parade of puppies and dogs joyfully transitioned from our nurturing foster care into their loving, permanent homes. Each successful adoption marks a heartwarming chapter in our ongoing mission to provide rescue dogs with a fresh start and a brighter future.

About

Welcome to Paws for Alohas, a dedicated space for canine compassion and community support. Our mission is to connect loving foster homes with dogs in need, providing a nurturing temporary haven until they find their forever families. We believe every dog deserves a chance at a happy life, and through our website, we aim to make this a reality. Join us in our journey to bring joy and companionship to both dogs and humans alike, one tail wag at a time.
